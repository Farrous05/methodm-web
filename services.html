The MethodM Stack.

Five disciplines. One integrated methodology. Built around your operation.

Each discipline operates standalone or as part of an integrated engagement. Instead of fixed bundles, we configure what your operation actually needs.

Cost Control & Inventory Management

The core of everything. Know your numbers down to the last unit.

A. Cost Control Setup

  • Inventory items coding and categorization
  • Units and yield calculations
  • Recipe preparation, costing and system linking
  • Standardized forms: Transfers, Wastage, Staff Meals, Production
  • Preliminary menu engineering

B. Menu Costing & Engineering

  • Menu costing maintenance and updates
  • Profitability analysis by menu item
  • Pricing and performance recommendations

C. Inventory Control & Physical Counts

  • Physical inventory counts (weekly, monthly, annual)
  • Full counts across warehouses and outlets
  • Inventory movement monitoring and reconciliation

D. Monthly Cost Control (Ongoing)

  • Data entry: movements, production, wastage, invoices
  • Monthly costing tables with full cost ratios
  • Variance analysis: expected vs. actual
  • Periodic menu engineering and remote support

E. Operational Spot Checks

  • Weekly spot checks on selected inventory items
  • Stock verification against system records
  • Findings documentation and recommendations

Financial & Accounting Services

Clean books. Accurate reports. No surprises.

A. Accounting Setup

  • Accounting system implementation and configuration
  • Company accounting structure setup
  • Custom financial reporting design

B. Accounting & Data Entry (Ongoing)

  • Operational entries: invoices, stock movements, expenses, payments
  • Accounts payable and receivable management
  • Monthly bank and ledger reconciliations

C. Financial Reporting

  • Financial statements preparation
  • P&L reporting (monthly, quarterly, annual)

D. Budget Preparation

  • Revenue, cost, and cash flow budget development
  • Budget model validation and finalization

E. Variance Analysis

  • Review of expected vs. actual costs
  • Key issue identification and improvement recommendations

Purchasing & Supply Chain Optimization

Smarter buying. Better margins. Less exposure.

A. Supplier Management

B. Purchasing Operations

  • Daily ordering process execution

  • Invoice, credit note, and rebate management

C. Procurement Optimization

  • Bi-weekly product sampling and tasting
  • Market surveys and supplier comparisons
  • PAR (Periodic Automatic Replenishment) management
